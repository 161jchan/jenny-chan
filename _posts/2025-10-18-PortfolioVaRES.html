I'm excited to share my recent risk modeling project! I've put together a portfolio holding 100 shares of TSLA, 200 shares of NVDA, and a Short 50 shares of SPY. Using daily price data from January 2, 2023, to January 2, 2025, we'll use the Historical Simulation method to find the portfolio's 99% Value at Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES).

Daily PnL Calculation

For each day \(t\), the PnL for each asset is:

$$ \text{PnL}_{\text{Asset}, t} = (\text{Price}_t - \text{Price}_{t-1}) \times \text{Quantity} $$

The total portfolio PnL is the sum:

$$ \text{PnL}_t = \text{PnL}_{\text{TSLA}, t} + \text{PnL}_{\text{NVDA}, t} + \text{PnL}_{\text{SPY}, t} $$

Crucial Point: For the Short 50 SPY position, we use a negative quantity (-50). This means a price increase in SPY results in a loss (negative PnL), which is correct for a short.

$$ \text{PnL}_{\text{SPY}, t} = (\text{SPY}_t - \text{SPY}_{t-1}) \times \mathbf{(-50)} $$

Step 1: Build the Historical PnL Vector

We calculate the PnL for each of the ~500 trading days in our dataset. This gives us a vector of 500+ simulated daily PnL outcomes, which forms the empirical distribution of our portfolio's returns.

Step 2: Calculate the 99% Value at Risk (VaR)

The 99% VaR is the loss level we expect to exceed only 1% of the time. Using our historical PnL vector, we find the 1st percentile (the 0.01 quantile). In Excel, with our PnL data in cells B2:B505:

$$ \mathbf{99\%\ VaR} = \mathbf{ABS}(\text{PERCENTILE.INC}(\text{B2:B505}, \mathbf{0.01})) $$

We take the absolute value to report VaR as a positive dollar amount representing the loss.

Step 3: Calculate the 99% Expected Shortfall (ES)

Expected Shortfall goes a step further, telling us the average loss on the worst 1% of days. We calculate it by averaging all the PnLs that are worse than the VaR threshold.

If our calculated VaR (the negative threshold) is in cell C1:

$$ \mathbf{99\%\ ES} = \mathbf{ABS}(\text{AVERAGEIF}(\text{B2:B505}, "\mathbf{<}" \& \mathbf{C1})) $$

The Results

After running the calculations on the two years of data, here are the results:

  • 99% Value at Risk (VaR): $2,785
  • 99% Expected Shortfall (ES): $3,739.90

Interpretation

Based on the market behavior from 2023 to 2025:

  • On 99% of days, the maximum loss for this portfolio is expected to be **$2,785** or less.
  • On the worst 1% of days (when losses exceed $2,785), the average loss is significantly higher, at **$3,740**.

The fact that the ES is substantially larger than the VaR indicates that when this portfolio has a bad day, the losses can be quite severe. The short SPY position adds a unique risk dynamic, as it profits when the market falls but amplifies losses when the market rallies sharply.

This analysis gives a clear, data-driven view of the potential downside in a concentrated, hedged portfolio like this one.
